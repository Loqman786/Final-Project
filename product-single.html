A turbo, short for turbocharger, is a device used in internal combustion engines to increase the efficiency and power output of the engine. It works by using exhaust gases to spin a turbine, which is connected to a compressor. This compressor then pressurizes the intake air going into the engine, allowing it to burn more fuel and produce more power. In simple terms, a turbocharger forces more air into the engine, allowing it to burn more fuel and generate more power without increasing the engine's size. This is commonly used in high-performance vehicles and is also becoming more common in smaller engines to improve fuel efficiency without sacrificing power.
